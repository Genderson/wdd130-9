Services

Training

Training an animal consists of teaching your pet to respond in a concrete way to a verbal command or gestural signal. Since the line that separates training from education is very fine, we explain the main differences between the two concepts. Canine and feline training is more than teaching your dog or cat tricks, making it sit, lie down or give you a paw, it consists of guiding your pet, educating it to improve coexistence and strengthen its bond. To do this, it is recommended to use positive canine and feline training techniques that motivate the animal and focus on guaranteeing its well-being. With us you will learn to educate your dog or cat by understanding, first, canine and feline behavior, discovering how to interpret their language and the signals they emit to indicate all their emotions. On our page you will find all the necessary information on behavior modification, basic commands, exercises, activities and training techniques.

Differences between educating and training

The objective of educating a pet is to guide it during its learning from a young age so that it learns to behave and adapt in different daily situations, thus making it feel safe and calm. The training is intended to teach him to perform a specific action after a command.

The use of positive and negative reinforcement

During the learning process with our pet, methods are used to establish an association between a stimulus and the response produced by the animal through conditioning. At the same time, it's about figuring out your pet's needs and abilities to nurture them.

Positive reinforcement

It consists of giving your pet a stimulus that really motivates him, such as a prize in the form of food, play, affectionate words, caresses, when he performs the desired behavior. In this way, your pet will repeat the action to receive the prize again.

negative reinforcement

It is based on removing an annoying or repulsive stimulus from your pet. For example, in the case of dogs, stretch the leash against the ground so that they lie down because, as soon as they do, they will stop feeling pressure. The dog will repeat the behavior again to avoid or run away from that unpleasant stimulus.
